# GENERAL

## General

### Background

#### In general, ships use generators as the primary generating source, and in recent years, due to technological developments in battery systems and fuel cell systems and the strengthening of environmental regulations, the application of hybrid electric power systems combining generators, battery systems, fuel cell systems, and other similar technologies has been increasing. Through such hybrid electric power systems, the operational efficiency and economic performance of ships can be improved.

#### By applying hybrid electric power systems to ships, benefits such as enhanced safety and reliability, improved operational efficiency, reduced fuel consumption, and reduced environmental footprint can be expected.

### Purpose

#### The purpose of these Guidelines is to provide test requirements and technical information necessary for ships intending to apply onboard hybrid electric power systems in order to ensure the safety and reliability of such systems.

### Application

#### These Guidelines apply to ships designed, constructed, or converted with the application of hybrid electric power systems composed of two or more different types of generating sources or stored electrical energy.

#### These Guidelines apply to the design, configuration, and integration of hybrid electric power systems, their subsystems, and related machinery and equipment.

#### For requirements not specified in these Guidelines, the relevant provisions of the Rules for the Classification of Steel Ships shall be referred to. For example, generators are subject to Pt 6 Ch 1 Sec 3 of the Rules; battery systems are subject to the Guidance for Battery Systems Onboard Ships;, fuel cell systems are subject to the Guidance for Fuel Cell Systems on Board Ships;, and photovoltaic systems are subject to the Guidance for Photovoltaic Systems Onboard Ships.

#### The hybrid electric power systems addressed in these Guidelines do not include combinations of generators and shaft generators. For requirements related to parallel operation between onboard generators and shaft generators, reference shall be made to the Guidelines for Shaft Generator Systems.

### Definitions

Unless otherwise defined in these Guidelines, the definitions of terms shall be in accordance with the Rules for the Classification of Steel Ships.

#### **Hybrid electric power system** means a shipboard power system in which internal combustion engine driven generators are provided as the primary generating source and are combined with energy storage devices and/or energy transformation devices.

#### **All-electric power system** means a shipboard power system consisting exclusively of energy storage devices and/or energy transformation devices. Internal combustion engine driven generators are permitted only for emergency power supply.

#### **All-electric ship** means a ship capable of supplying electrical power required for the operation of the vessel, including propulsion, solely by means of an all-electric power system.

#### **Electric energy storage system** (hereinafter referred to as "ESS") means a system consisting of energy storage devices, power conversion devices (where applicable), control systems, and associated components, which is used to supply electrical power to the ship's electric propulsion system and/or main power distribution system. Battery systems may be configured as one type of electric energy storage system.

#### **Energy transformation device** (hereinafter referred to as "ETD") means a device that converts one form of energy into another form of energy. Fuel cell systems and photovoltaic systems may be configured as types of electric energy transformation devices.

#### **Energy management system** (hereinafter referred to as "EMS") means a higher-level management system of ESS or ETD that includes functions for monitoring and controlling energy capacity. Where the functions of an energy management system are incorporated within a power management system, the relevant power management system shall be regarded as the energy management system.

#### **Zero emission mode**

##### ZE mode (hereinafter referred to as "ZE mode") means an operating condition of a hybrid electric power system in which the ESS and/or ETD temporarily become the sole source of power connected to the electrical power system.

##### In this mode, all onboard generating sets, main engines, boilers and incinerators (where applicable), may be shut down, and no associated exhaust gas emissions are produced for a specified period of time.

#### **Blackout prevention mode** (hereinafter referred to as "BP mode") means an operating mode in which the ESS is continuously connected to the main power distribution system and is capable of supplying electrical power immediately when one of the main power sources fails or is shut down.

#### **Power support mode** (hereinafter referred to as "PS mode") means an operating mode that provides one of the power support functions defined below (see Figure 1).

##### Load smoothing

###### The load smoothing function is a function in which the ESS repeatedly charges and discharges in order to maintain the load of operating generating sets within predefined permissible limits while compensating for variations in power system load.

###### This function may contribute to optimizing fuel consumption and reducing exhaust gas emissions by limiting load fluctuations of the generating sets.

##### Peak shaving: The peak shaving function is intended to respond to short-term increases in power demand. By supplying power peaks arising from highly fluctuating loads using the ESS, the starting or additional connection of generating sets may be avoided.

##### Enhanced dynamic response: The enhanced dynamic response function is primarily applied to gas-fuelled or dual-fuel generating sets. When the load increases or decreases rapidly, the ESS immediately supplies deficient power or absorbs excess power through charging in order to improve the dynamic response characteristics of the generating sets. In the case of dual-fuel engines, this function may prevent a changeover to fuel oil caused by rapid load variations.

### Equivalency

Additional considerations or requirements may be applied depending on the technical characteristics of the hybrid electric power system. Designs that differ from the requirements of these Guidelines may be accepted, provided that an equivalent level of safety is demonstrated and subject to evaluation and approval by the Society.

# CLASSIFICATION SURVEYS

## General

### General

#### Manufacturing, testing and inspection and associated documentation shall comply with the test specifications and recognized standards required by these Guidelines.

#### With respect to hybrid electric power systems and all-electric power systems, manufacturing, test and inspection items not specified in this Section are to comply with the applicable requirements of Rules for the Classification of Steel Ships and relevant Guidance.

## Type Approval

### Type approval

#### Hybrid electric power systems and their components are to be subject to type approval.

#### The scope of type approval is to be in accordance with the following Guidance, as applicable:

##### Battery systems: Guidance for Battery Systems Onboard Ships

##### Power conversion devices: Ch 3, Sec 39 of the Guidance for Manufacturing Approval and Type Approval

##### Fuel cell systems: Guidance for Fuel Cell Systems on Board Ships

##### Energy management systems: Ch 3, Sec 23 of the Guidance on Manufacturing Approval and Type Approval

##### Photovoltaic systems: Guidance for Photovoltaic Systems Onboard Ships

## Commissioning Test

### General

#### Procedures for tests after installation are to be submitted to the Society.

#### In addition to the test requirements specified in the applicable Rules and Guidance for each power source, hybrid electric power systems and all-electric power systems are to undergo the test requirements specified in 302. Where deemed necessary by the Society, additional test items or modified test requirements may be required.

### Tests after installation

#### The following items are to be verified onboard after installation, as a minimum:

##### Verification of normal operation of the monitoring system.

##### Verification of proper operation of alarms, fault indications, and associated functions, including interfaces with other ship systems.

##### Verification of power quality under various operating modes in accordance with Pt 6, Ch 1, 306, (4) of the Rules.

##### Verification of the disconnection function of the ESS or ETD and the automatic starting function of the standby power source, where applicable.

##### During parallel operation of multiple power sources, verification that the load is transferred progressively in accordance with the maximum electrical step load characteristics of each power source and that the load is evenly shared among the power sources.

#### **ZE mode test**

ZE mode shall be verified by performing the following test requirements:

##### Load discharge test of ESS and measurement of the continuous operating time until the State of Charge (SoC) reaches its lower limit.

##### Verification of automatic starting of standby power sources upon failure of the ESS or when the SoC reaches its lower limit.

##### Performance of a charging test to verify that the ESS is normally charged in accordance with the specified conditions after termination of the ZE mode (refer to Ch 3, 205.)

#### **BP mode test**

BP mode shall be verified by performing the following tests while the mode is activated:

##### Verification that the ESS is automatically connected to the main switchboard to supply power upon failure or stoppage of an operating generating source.

##### Measurement of the ESS sole operation time following (1), where applicable.

##### Verification of the automatic starting of standby generating sources upon failure of the ESS or when the SoC reaches its lower limit

##### Performance of a charging test to verify that the ESS is normally charged in accordance with specified conditions after termination of BP mode (refer to Ch 3, 205).

#### **PS mode test**

PS mode shall be verified by performing the following tests:

##### Verification of load smoothing

###### It shall be verified that the ESS supplies power to the grid or is charged in response to load increase and decrease according to the preset values.

###### Standby generating sources shall be started automatically when the load demand exceeds the rated output of the ESS.

###### The operation of the ESS is to be verified for at least four(4) hours under normal operating conditions. However, the minimum duration of the continuous operation test may be adjusted in accordance with the vessel's defined operating profile.

##### Verification of peak shaving

###### During generator operation, it is to be verified that the ESS compensates for transient loads caused by the starting heavy loads.

###### Standby generating sources shall start automatically when the load demand exceeds the rated output of the ESS.

##### Verification of dynamic response improvement

###### During gas fuel supply of gas engines or dual-fuel engines, it shall be verified that the ESS supplies power to the grid or is charged in response to load increase and decrease according to the preset values.

###### Standby generating sources shall start automatically or the fuel mode shall automatically change to oil fuel mode when the load demand exceeds the supply capacity of the ESS.

###### The operation of the ESS is to be verified for at least four(4) hours under normal operating conditions. However, the minimum duration of the continuous operation test may be adjusted in accordance with the vessel's defined operating profile.

# SYSTEMS AND EQUIPMENT

## General

### General

#### For all operating modes of the hybrid power system, the capacity of the ESS and/or ETD shall be selected to ensure continuous power supply to essential and habitability services, even when one generating source is maintained as a standby condition. However, these requirements may be modified for ships operating in restricted areas recognized by the Society.

#### All-electric power systems shall consist of a combination of ESS and/or ETD and be designed to supply power to the onboard loads.

#### Where the main source of electrical power in an all-electric power system is based solely on ESS or ETD, it is recommended that the main source consists of at least two independent ESSs or ETDs, located in different spaces. However, these requirements may be modified for ships operating in restricted areas recognized by the Society.

#### Hybrid power systems and all-electric power systems may be configured as follows, depending on system requirements, operational purposes, and the vessel's operational profiles.

### Operating modes

#### System performance objectives for each operating mode of hybrid power systems and all-electric power systems shall be defined in advance and agreed upon between the designer, owner, and relevant parties.

#### Drawings and data describing integrated sub-systems and designed operating modes shall be included in relevant documents, such as design criteria, specifications, or operation manuals. Furthermore, key parameter settings applied during each operating mode and its transition shall be clearly identifiable in the associated technical documentation.

### Hybrid power system performance

#### The power supply of the switchboard may transiently deviate from the specified power quality limits due to the compensation operation of power conversion devices supplying power to essential services. However, electrical equipment shall be designed to operate normally for a specified duration under such transient conditions, even if the power quality momentarily exceeds the permissible limits.

#### Where two or more generating sources are installed to operate a hybrid power system, a power management system shall be provided. The power management system shall include functions for automatic starting, synchronization, parallel operation, and load sharing.

#### ESS and ETD shall be configured to ensure continuity of power supply through protection and control functions, even in the event of a failure of a single generating source or power conversion device during parallel operation with generators.

#### The ESS shall be capable of being charged via the ship's electrical installation while at quay.

#### Short-circuit current contributions from the ESS and ETD shall be considered in short-circuit current calculations. When calculating the maximum prospective short-circuit current, the maximum combination of generators that may be connected simultaneously and the maximum loads normally connected at the same time shall be considered.

#### In ESS charging mode, the maximum charging current of the batteries shall be taken into account.

### Control and instrumentation of ESS and ETD

#### It shall be possible to stop the ESS and ETD from the engine control room. Upon such operation, the standby power source shall start automatically if required.

#### The following information shall be continuously displayed in the engine control room:

##### Active operating modes or functions (ZE mode, PS mode, load leveling, peak shaving, dynamic response enhancement)

##### Charge/discharge status of the ESS

##### State of Charge (SOC) of the ESS

##### Remaining available operating time during ZE mode and BP mode operation

##### State of Health (SOH) of the ESS

##### Current and voltage values of the ESS

##### Current and voltage values for each battery pack unit

##### Active power supplied

#### For ships without an engine control room, the requirements in 1. and 2. shall be met at the navigation control station on bridge.
